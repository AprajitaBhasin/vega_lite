Fires from Space: Australia

This visualisation will analyse the fires in Australia recognised from space from 01/08/2019 to 30/09/2019. It will use the fire locations processed by the University of Maryland and the thermal anomalies captured by NASA FIRMS MODIS.

It will mainly use the data obtained on variables such as brightness, date, location
fire radiative power, part of the day the fire occurred and the level of
confidence (%) that the fire took place to not only analyse but also
retrospectively investigate if the findings via the satellite are in
line with expections.

A geographical representation of Australian fires from space
This graph geographically maps the fires throughout the period outlined with the
colour revealing the brightness of the fires. Furthermore, the fires can be
filtered according to the dates to reveal the brightness and the count
of those respective brightness bins.
The geographical map reveal that the majority of the fires recognised from space
are centred around high vegetation areas. Furthermore, the high count of fires is
in line with expectations.


An analysis of FRP, brightness and confidence
This zoomable graph shows the relationship between the variables: confidence, brightness,
day/night and FRP. The fire with the highest FRP has been highlighted.
This graph shows how the confidence increases as the FRP and brightness of the fire increases.
Additionally, on average, more biomass is being consumed during the day with higher brightness
and confidence levels. This is hand with the date of the highlighted point,
makes the data in line with expectations.
