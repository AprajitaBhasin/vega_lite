Due to the upcoming bushfire season in Australia, it is important to have a look
at historical data to understand the high-risk areas, the number of fires that occurred
and the brightness distribution. Furthermore, as the reliance on technology
is increasing, it is important to take a look at the data acquired from the satellites
and confirm if they are in line with our expectations.

Thus, this visualisation will analyse the fires in Australia recognised from space from 01/08/2019 to 30/09/2019. It will use the fire locations processed by the University of Maryland and the thermal anomalies captured by NASA FIRMS MODIS.

It will mainly use the data obtained on variables such as brightness, date, location
fire radiative power, part of the day the fire occurred and the level of
confidence (%) that the fire took place to not only analyse but also
retrospectively investigate if the findings via the satellite are in
line with expectations.

A geographical representation of Australian fires from space
This graph geographically maps the fires throughout the period outlined with the
colour revealing the brightness of the fires. It will identify the high-risk areas
and if they are in-line with expectations.

Furthermore, the fires can be filtered according to the dates to reveal the brightness
and the count of those respective brightness bins.
The geographical map reveals that the majority of the fires recognised from space
are centred around high vegetation areas. Furthermore, the high count of fires is
in line with expectations.



An analysis of FRP, brightness and confidence
This zoomable graph shows the relationship between the variables: confidence, brightness,
day/night and FRP. The fire with the highest FRP has been highlighted, and
the red lines represent the means of their axes. It investigates if the
relationship between these variables is logically correct to confirm
that the measurements are appropriate. This is an important test if we are to completely rely
on the satellites for measurements of future bushfire seasons.
This graph shows how the confidence increases as the FRP and brightness of the fire increases.
Additionally, on average, more biomass is being consumed during the day with higher brightness
and confidence levels. This is hand with the date of the highlighted point,
makes the data in line with expectations.
Mean FRP across the time period
This graph compares the mean FRP of the dataset to previous years to identify the anomalous fires.
This aids in identifying the conditions which led to higher than normal readings for future reference.
